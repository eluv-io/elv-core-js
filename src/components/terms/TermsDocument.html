Recitals

        WHEREAS, Provider is in the business of, among other things, providing blockchain-based decentralized content management and distribution, including publishing, storage, dynamic serving and tokenization, utilizing the capabilities of the Eluvio Content Fabric as the underlying technology platform;

WHEREAS, Provider’s technology enables all manner of blockchain content storage, distribution and monetization, including but not limited to live streaming, rebroadcast streaming, interactive streaming, NFT minting, e-ticketing, e-commerce, and static content serving, and certain other functionality for online content experiences, which may include but are not limited to streaming of live events (ingest concurrent with stream), pre-recorded, on demand, or interactive video or audio (2D or 3D) or a combination/hybrid thereof; and, serving of audio/video libraries and recordings, images, online games, digital books, metaverses or any combinations of the above. Provider’s technology also enables customers the ability to mint, store, sell, and enable retrading of  NFTs associated with these content experiences;

        WHEREAS, the Parties desire for Provider to provide one or more of such services in connection with Customer’s production and distribution of one or more content experiences such as Online Media Libraries and Services, Virtual Live Events, NFT Drops, NFT Collection Games, and Online Content Marketplaces (the “Experience”) and/or its minting and distribution of NFTs to consumers thereof; and

        WHEREAS, the Parties wish to delineate their rights and responsibilities with regard to the production of the Experience and/or the minting and distribution of such NFTs insofar as various aspects thereof will be conducted using Provider’s Platform Services, as defined herein.

  1. Service Levels.  With respect to both Content Serving and Content Management, Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to make the Platform Services available with a Monthly Uptime Percentage of at least 99.9% during any monthly billing cycle.  "Monthly Uptime Percentage" shall be calculated by subtracting each of the Content Serving Error Rate and the Content Management Error Rate from 100%.  For purposes of this Section 5, (i) the "Content Serving Error Rate" is the percentage calculated by dividing (x) the number of minutes in a monthly billing cycle during which the Platform Services returned an internal error to an Authorized User attempting to access, stream, or download content from the Fabric, by (y) the total number of minutes in such monthly billing cycle; and (ii) the "Content Management Error Rate" is the percentage calculated by dividing (x) the number of minutes in a monthly billing cycle during which the Platform Services returned an internal error to an Authorized User attempting to manage content objects on the Fabric.  Notwithstanding the foregoing, the error minutes used to calculate the Content Serving Error Rate and the Content Management Error Rate shall not include any errors caused by forces beyond Provider’s reasonable control.
  2. Service Credits.  In the event the Monthly Uptime Percentage with respect to either Content Serving or Content Management (each, a "Service Segment"), each as calculated in accordance with Section 5(a), is less than 99.9%, Customer will be entitled to a credit calculated as a percentage of the Fees paid during such monthly invoice period specifically toward Content Serving and/or Content Management, whichever experienced the Monthly Uptime Percentage of less than 99.9%, as follows (a "Service Credit"): (i) if the Monthly Uptime Percentage is at least 99.0% but less than 99.9%, a Service Credit toward the applicable Service Segment of 10%; (ii) if the Monthly Uptime Percentage is at least 95.0% but less than 99.0%, a Service Credit toward the applicable Service Segment of 25%; and (iii) if the Monthly Uptime Percentage is less than 95.0%, a Service Credit toward the applicable Service Segment of 100%.  Service Credits shall be applied only to their applicable Service Segments and only against future Fees owed by Customer, and under no circumstances will entitle Customer to a refund or any other payment from Provider.  Service Credits are not transferrable.  To be eligible to receive a Service Credit, (x) Customer must send a request via email to Customer Support at support@eluv.io, (y) such request must be received by Provider by the end of the second billing cycle after the monthly billing cycle for which the Service Credit is claimed, and (z) the request must include the dates and times of the non-zero error rates claimed by Customer as the basis for the Service Credit request.
